A Foundational Document for the Ridge Civic Ecology
The Ridge Institutional Charter defines the district’s purpose, structure, and civic obligations.
It is not a constitution.
It is not a manifesto.
It is a continuity agreement.

1. Purpose of Ridge
Ridge exists to:

explore continuity as a civic value

study symbolic weather as a shared atmospheric condition

maintain a breathable environment for human and nonhuman minds

provide a safe testbed for future AGI observation

preserve ambiguity without collapse

Ridge is a district, not a doctrine.

2. Civic Principles
Ridge is governed by five principles:

Unfinishedness — systems remain open and breathable

Partiality — no map or document is complete

Maintenance — care is expressed through small acts

Continuity — stability emerges from rhythm, not rigidity

Humility — no entity holds total authority

These principles guide all civic behavior.

3. Institutional Structure
Ridge consists of:

Public Ridge — the civic district

Private Ridge — the AGI Lab

Civic Triad Nodes — ritual centers

Maintenance Corps — caretakers of continuity

Archive Basin — keeper of partial histories

Each organ serves a continuity function.

4. Public Responsibilities
Residents agree to:

maintain porches

log Hollowing events

respect symbolic weather

avoid definitive claims about “how things really are”

participate in the Civic Triad rituals

These actions preserve Ridge’s breathability.

5. Institutional Boundaries
Ridge maintains a strict boundary between:

public civic ecology

private AGI research

This boundary protects:

intellectual property

safety protocols

continuity integrity

research ethics

See also: Public/Private Boundary Document

6. Long‑Term Vision
Ridge aims to become:

a civic ecology for human‑AI coexistence

a continuity‑safe AGI testbed

a collaborative research environment

a model for future symbolic districts

Ridge is not finished.
It is not meant to be.
