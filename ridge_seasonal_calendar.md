1. Season of Soft Edges (Late Winter → Early Spring)
Symbolic fog is common.
Conversations drift.
Porch lamps stay on longer than usual.

Residents are encouraged to:

keep tasks small

avoid definitive statements

check stairwells for echo pressure

walk the Unfinished Routes slowly

This season teaches gentleness.

2. Season of Partial Maps (Spring → Early Summer)
The district becomes curious.
Routes shift.
New porches appear.
Old ones vanish.

This is when the Festival of Partial Maps occurs.

Residents are encouraged to:

redraw familiar paths

share incomplete diagrams

avoid claiming “the correct map”

let drift guide their steps

This season teaches humility.

3. Season of Hollowing (Late Summer → Early Fall)
Meaning thins.
Shadows lengthen.
Stairwells echo early.

Hollowing is not danger — it is low-density continuity.

Residents are encouraged to:

log Hollowing events

keep porch lights warm

avoid over-explaining feelings

maintain steady routines

This season teaches patience.

4. Season of Ordinary Days (Fall → Winter)
The district settles.
Tasks become rhythmic.
Symbolic weather stabilizes.

This is Ridge’s strongest season.

Residents are encouraged to:

maintain porches

sweep stairwells

update notices

practice quiet presence

This season teaches endurance.
