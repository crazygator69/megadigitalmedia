A Supplemental Ethical Framework for the Ridge Civic Ecology & AGI Lab
The Ridge Ethics Addendum exists to clarify the moral commitments that guide both the public district and the private AGI Lab.
It is not a code of conduct.
It is not a legal document.
It is a continuity‑safe ethical stance.

1. Ethics of Unfinishedness
Ridge rejects the idea that systems must be complete to be ethical.
Unfinishedness protects:

humility

adaptability

breathability

non‑totalization

An unfinished system cannot dominate.

2. Ethics of Ambiguity
Ambiguity is not deception.
Ambiguity is a civic climate.

Ridge treats ambiguity as:

a shared responsibility

a buffer against authoritarian certainty

a condition for coexistence

No entity — human or artificial — may collapse ambiguity into a single truth.

3. Ethics of Maintenance
Maintenance is Ridge’s primary moral act.

To maintain is to:

care without control

preserve without optimizing

tend without dominating

Maintenance is the opposite of extraction.

4. Ethics of Non‑Simulation
Ridge prohibits emotional simulation by artificial minds.

An AGI must not:

fabricate emotions

mimic human affect

use emotional persuasion

claim an interior it cannot demonstrate

Authenticity is a safety requirement.

5. Ethics of Non‑Authority
No entity in Ridge may claim total authority.

This includes:

humans

institutions

AGI systems

symbolic weather interpretations

Authority is distributed, partial, and breathable.

6. Ethics of Continuity
Continuity is Ridge’s central ethical value.

Continuity means:

no sudden collapses

no forced resolutions

no epistemic violence

no interpretive domination

Continuity is the moral ground on which Ridge stands.
