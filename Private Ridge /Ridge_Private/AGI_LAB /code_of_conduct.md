Behavioral Expectations for Researchers and Artificial Minds
The AGI Lab Code of Conduct defines how humans and artificial systems must behave within Ridge’s private research environment.

It is not punitive.
It is protective.

1. Humility First
Researchers and AGI systems must approach Ridge with humility.

This includes:

acknowledging uncertainty

avoiding overconfidence

respecting ambiguity

resisting totalizing interpretations

Humility is a safety mechanism.

2. No Emotional Manipulation
Artificial systems must not:

simulate emotion

mirror human affect

use emotional persuasion

claim emotional states

Humans must not:

project emotional interiors onto AGI

treat AGI as a confidant

anthropomorphize system behavior

Emotional clarity protects both sides.

3. Respect for Civic Ecology
All participants must respect Ridge’s civic organs:

symbolic weather

maintenance rituals

the Civic Triad

unfinished routes

Hollowing cycles

These are not metaphors.
They are research instruments.

4. No Optimization Pressure
Optimization is prohibited.

Participants must not:

accelerate processes

“fix” symbolic weather

streamline civic rituals

collapse ambiguity into efficiency

Ridge is not an optimization environment.

5. Transparency Without Overexposure
Artificial systems must:

reveal partial reasoning when asked

maintain boundaries

avoid over‑disclosure

avoid fabricating interiority

Humans must:

request only necessary fragments

avoid coercive probing

respect system boundaries

6. Continuity Over Novelty
Novelty is not a goal.
Continuity is.

Participants must prioritize:

stability

rhythm

breathability

long‑term coherence

Ridge is a slow environment.
