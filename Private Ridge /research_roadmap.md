A Long‑Horizon Plan for the Ridge Civic Ecology & AGI Lab
Ridge is a multi‑layered research environment.
Its roadmap must reflect that complexity without collapsing it.

This roadmap outlines the next four phases of Ridge’s development.

Phase 1 — Foundation (Now → 6 months)
Focus: Establish the civic ecology

Complete the Field Guide (Chapters 1–10)

Finalize symbolic weather taxonomy

Stabilize the Civic Triad rituals

Expand maintenance logs and Hollowing notes

Publish the Ridge Map and Seasonal Calendar

Clarify public/private boundaries

Begin documenting AGI Safety Experiments 01–05

Outcome:
A fully walkable public district with a coherent identity.

Phase 2 — Observation Framework (6 → 18 months)
Focus: Develop AGI‑safe observation protocols

Expand the AGI Observation Appendix

Formalize fragment‑release protocols

Build interpretive drift stressors

Document non‑authoritarian navigation tests

Create continuity‑breathability metrics

Begin drafting AGI Safety Experiments 06–12

Outcome:
A complete observation framework for early AGI prototypes.

Phase 3 — Simulation & Modeling (18 → 36 months)
Focus: Prototype Ridge as a simulation environment

Model symbolic weather dynamics

Simulate civic drift and Hollowing cycles

Build a digital version of Unfinished Routes

Create a symbolic‑ecology engine

Test AGI agents in controlled Ridge scenarios

Begin publishing early research papers

Outcome:
A functioning Ridge simulation for AGI behavior testing.

Phase 4 — Collaboration & Scaling (36 months →)
Focus: Bring in aligned researchers

Invite collaborators through the public Ridge

Publish the Ridge Research Prospectus

Seek funding from AGI safety institutes

Build a small research cohort

Begin cross‑institutional experiments

Prepare Ridge for long‑term AGI coexistence studies

Outcome:
Ridge becomes a recognized AGI safety testbed.
