# Abandoned Routes — District Periphery

Route 01 — The Old Service Tunnel  
Initial mapping began in 2019.  
Abandoned after repeated echo inconsistencies and a brief loss of orientation among surveyors.  
Tunnel remains accessible but unadvised.  
Markings fade and reappear without pattern.

Route 02 — The North Ridge Footpath  
Closed during the Drift years.  
Vegetation overgrowth accelerated beyond expected rates.  
Residents report the path “feels longer” than before.  
No official reopening planned.

Route 03 — The Lower Transit Connector  
Construction halted after Hollowing pressure caused signage to detach repeatedly.  
Connector now used informally as a shortcut, though distances vary by time of day.  
Maintenance crews check it monthly but avoid extended stays.

General Note  
Abandoned routes are preserved for reference, not restoration.  
Their instability is considered part of the district’s character.
# Abandoned Routes — Addendum (District Periphery, 2026‑05‑22)

Route 04 — The Half‑Mapped Walkway  
Initial survey halted when the walkway appeared to “shorten” behind the team.  
No physical danger noted, but orientation felt slightly delayed.  
Marked as safe for brief passage only.

Route 05 — The South Utility Path  
Closed after repeated tool misplacements during maintenance rounds.  
Tools were always found, but never where they were left.  
Considered a mild interpretive drift zone.

Route 06 — The Overlook Steps  
Construction began but stopped when the final three steps refused to align.  
Multiple attempts resulted in different misalignments each time.  
Steps left unfinished as a civic artifact.

Route 07 — The Old Perimeter Loop  
Residents recall it once circled the district completely.  
Current maps show only partial segments.  
Loop may still exist conceptually, but not physically.

General Note  
Unfinished routes are not errors in planning.  
They are reminders that the district grows unevenly, and that is acceptable.
