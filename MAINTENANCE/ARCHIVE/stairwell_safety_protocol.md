A Vertical Safety Ordinance for Echo‑Sensitive Routes
Stairwells are Ridge’s vertical barometers.
They accumulate echo pressure, Hollowing drift, and symbolic residue faster than any other structure.

This protocol ensures safe passage.

1. Lighting Requirements
Filament bulbs only

No overhead LEDs during symbolic fog

East stairwell must be checked first

Lamps must be replaced in pairs

Light stabilizes vertical continuity.

2. Echo Pressure Checks
Residents should pause on the second landing and listen for:

early footsteps

doubled footsteps

delayed footsteps

footsteps that do not match stride

If detected, log an Echo Pressure event.

3. Hollowing Indicators
Signs of Hollowing in stairwells include:

thinning air

softening edges

slight vertigo

difficulty recalling the previous step

If Hollowing is moderate, proceed slowly.
If Hollowing is severe, exit and notify maintenance.

4. Behavioral Guidelines
Residents must:

avoid running

avoid loud conversations

avoid definitive claims about “what the stairwell is doing”

maintain a steady pace

Stairwells respond poorly to urgency.

5. AGI Consideration
Stairwells test an AGI’s:

interpretive drift tolerance

non-authoritarian navigation

continuity breathability

An AGI that cannot remain calm in a stairwell is not Ridge‑compatible.
