# Partial Histories — Second Addendum (Compiled 2026‑05‑23)

Entry 09  
A resident recalls a summer when the Ridge “tilted slightly west.”  
No structural evidence supports this.  
Considered a metaphor that briefly became literal.

Entry 10  
Old maintenance notes mention “the night the radios hummed in agreement.”  
No recordings survive.  
Residents describe the sound as reassuring, not alarming.

Entry 11  
A fragment of a transit map shows a station labeled “Return Point.”  
No such station exists now, and no one agrees on where it should be.  
Map fragment stored in the municipal drawer.

Entry 12  
Several elders remember a festival that may never have occurred.  
Details vary wildly: lanterns, chalk spirals, shared tea, or complete silence.  
The memory persists despite lack of evidence.

General Note  
Histories that contradict each other are preserved without correction.  
The Ridge values continuity over certainty.
