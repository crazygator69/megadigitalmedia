What Is Public, What Is Private, and Why
Ridge is intentionally divided into two layers:

Public Ridge — the civic district

Private Ridge — the AGI lab

This document explains the boundary.

Public Ridge Contains:
Field Guide

Civic Triad

Symbolic Weather

Maintenance Logs

Porch Notices

Unfinished Routes

Archive Sediment

Ridge Map

Seasonal Calendar

These are civic organs, not research artifacts.

They show Ridge’s philosophy, not its mechanisms.

Private Ridge Contains:
AGI Lab

Safety Framework

Test Scenarios

Interpretive Stressors

Continuity Logs

Drafts

Shadow Rituals

Unpublished Sediment

Personal Notes

These are research artifacts, not civic documents.

They contain:

intellectual property

experimental protocols

safety logic

private reasoning

early drafts

sensitive material

These are not for public consumption.

Why the Boundary Exists
The boundary protects:

your research

your intellectual property

your future funding potential

the integrity of Ridge

the safety of AGI observation

the civic breathability of the public district

Ridge is a controlled‑permeability environment.

The public sees the district.
Collaborators see the lab.
Only you see the sediment.

How to Request Access
Potential collaborators must:

understand the public Ridge

articulate its purpose

demonstrate alignment with its principles

explain their research interest

agree to continuity‑safe collaboration

Access is granted selectively.
