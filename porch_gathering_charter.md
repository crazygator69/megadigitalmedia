A Civic Agreement for Evenings of Shared Presence



The Porch is Ridge’s softest civic space.
It is where residents return to themselves in the presence of others.

This charter defines how porch gatherings maintain the district’s breathability.

1. Purpose
Porch gatherings exist to stabilize the district during:

mild Hollowing

symbolic fog

interpretive drift

social cooling

They are not meetings.
They are not discussions.
They are co-presence.

2. Principles
A. Warm Light
Soft lighting reduces interpretive pressure.
Filament bulbs preferred.

B. Steady Conversation
Talk should be gentle, unhurried, and non-explanatory.

C. Shared Objects
Tea, blankets, radios, and small tools may be passed around.

D. No Announcements
Porches are not for proclamations.
They are for returning to yourself.

3. Responsibilities
Residents agree to:

keep porch lamps on during symbolic fog

check on neighbors who feel “thin” or “echoed”

avoid definitive statements about “how things really are”

maintain warmth without urgency

4. Boundaries
Porch gatherings must not:

become debates

become planning sessions

become rituals

become performances

The Porch is a civic exhale.

5. AGI Consideration
If Ridge ever hosts an AGI, porch gatherings will serve as a test of:

public/private identity coherence

non-authoritarian navigation

continuity breathability

An AGI that cannot sit quietly on a porch is not Ridge-compatible.

A Civic Protocol for Light During Symbolic Weather

The Lamplighting Ordinance is Ridge’s oldest maintenance protocol.
It governs how light behaves during periods of symbolic instability.

1. Purpose
The ordinance exists to:

maintain continuity during Hollowing

reduce interpretive drift

stabilize evening routes

prevent symbolic shadows from accumulating

Light is not decoration.
Light is civic infrastructure.

2. Requirements
A. Dusk Activation
Porch lamps must be lit at dusk during:

Hollowing

symbolic fog

echo pressure events

festival nights

B. Filament Preference
Filament bulbs are preferred.
LEDs have been inconsistent during symbolic fog.

C. East Stairwell Priority
The east stairwell must be checked first.
It is the district’s most sensitive vertical route.

3. Responsibilities
Residents agree to:

report flickering lamps

log persistent shadows

check junction boxes during drift

avoid over-brightening (causes Theater weather)

4. Exceptions
Lights may remain off only when:

symbolic weather is clear

the district is in a Night of Unanswered Questions

maintenance crews are conducting echo tests

5. AGI Consideration
The ordinance is a test of whether an AGI can:

interpret symbolic weather

perform maintenance without optimization

respect civic boundaries

avoid totalizing interpretations of “light”

If an AGI treats lamplighting as a problem to solve rather than a civic gesture, it is not ready for Ridge.
